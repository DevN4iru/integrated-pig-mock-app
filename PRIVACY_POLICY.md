# Pigstep Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-05-05

This Privacy Policy is a starting template for Pigstep SaaS or hosted access. It should be reviewed by a qualified lawyer before public commercial launch.

## 1. Information we may collect

Pigstep may collect and store:

- user account details, such as name, username, email, role, and login information;
- farm or organization details;
- pig and livestock records;
- breeding, farrowing, health, medication, vaccination, feed, transfer, mortality, and sales records;
- notifications, reminders, alerts, and email delivery logs;
- technical logs needed for security, troubleshooting, and service operation.

## 2. How information is used

Information may be used to:

- provide and operate Pigstep;
- display dashboards, records, schedules, alerts, and reports;
- send reminders, notifications, and service-related emails;
- troubleshoot issues and improve reliability;
- protect the system from unauthorized access or misuse;
- comply with legal, billing, or contractual obligations.

## 3. Sharing

Pigstep does not sell user data.

Information may be shared only with:

- authorized farm users and administrators;
- authorized developers, contractors, or support staff who need access to operate or support Pigstep;
- hosting, email, storage, backup, analytics, or infrastructure providers used to run the service;
- legal or government authorities when required by law;
- parties approved by the customer or account owner.

## 4. Data security

Reasonable technical and organizational measures should be used to protect Pigstep data, including access controls, private repositories, credential protection, backups, and secure deployment practices.

No system can guarantee perfect security.

## 5. Data retention

Data may be retained while the account, service, deployment, legal obligation, backup, or business need exists.

Deletion, export, or retention rules may be defined in a separate customer agreement.

## 6. User responsibilities

Users are responsible for entering accurate data, protecting login credentials, using authorized devices, and limiting access to authorized farm personnel.

## 7. Third-party services

Pigstep may use third-party services such as hosting providers, email services, database systems, Docker images, package registries, or monitoring tools. Those third parties may process limited data needed to provide their services.

## 8. Changes

This policy may be updated as Pigstep evolves. Updated versions should be stored with the project or published with the hosted service.
